Adam Sandler: Bayesian Pearson's Correlation

Description:

This data set, "Adam Sandler", provides Rotten Tomatoes ratings and box office success for movies featuring Adam Sandler.

The data contain the 2001-2015 filmography of Adam Sandler together with freshness ratings (indicated by a score from 0 to 1) and box office success in millions of US dollars. The data were obtained from the Rotten Tomatoes website.

Variables:

  1. Year - Year of the movies' premiere.
  2. Freshness - Freshness ratings on Rotten Tomatoes (ranges from 0 = rotten to 1 = fresh).
  3. Box Office ($M) - Box office succes of the movies, expressed in millions of US dollars.
  4. Movie Title - The movie title.

Thie example JASP file demonstrates the use of a Bayesian Pearson's correlation. Specifically, we will assess whether the data are more likely to occur under the null hypothesis (which holds that there is no linear association between freshness ratings and box office success), or under the alternative hypothesis (which holds that there is a positive association between freshness ratings and box office success, that is, Adam Sandler movies with relatively good reviews attract a bigger audience).

Reference:

The data were collected from Rotten Tomatoes (www.rottentomatoes.com) in 2015 by Eric-Jan Wagenmakers.

Bayesian Correlation

The sample correlation is weak and even somewhat negative (r = -0.029). The Bayes factor BF0+ indicates that it is about 5 times more likely that we would observe this sample correlation if there is no relationship between the ratings and Box Office sales than if the correlation was positive (and given by a uniform distribution from 0 to 1).
