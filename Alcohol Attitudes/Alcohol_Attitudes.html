Alcohol Attitudes: Factorial 3 x 3 Repeated Measures ANOVA

Description:

This data set, "Alcohol Attitudes", provides attitudes towards water, wine, and beer, after watching positive, neutral, or negative imagery of that drink - each participant features in each cell of the 3 x 3 design.

Variables:

  1. beerpos - Attitude towards beer after watching positive imagery.
  2. beerneg - Attitude towards beer after watching negative imagery.
  3. beerneut- Attitude towards beer after watching neutral imagery.
  4. winepos - Attitude towards wine after watching positive imagery.
  5. wineneg - Attitude towards a wine after watching negative imagery.
  6. wineneut - Attitude towards a wine after watching neutral imagery.
  7. waterpos - Attitude towards a water after watching positive imagery.
  8. waterneg - Attitude towards a water after watching negative imagery.
  9. waterneut - Attitude towards a water after watching neutral imagery.

Participants indicated their attitudes towards the drink on a scale from -100 to 100.

This example JASP file demonstrates the use of repeated measures ANOVA. Specifically, we will test whether participants have different attitudes towards different kinds of drink, and whether the attitudes can be manipulated by valence of the imagery.

Reference:

Field, A. P. (2017). Discovering Statistics Using IBM SPSS Statistics (5th ed.). London: Sage.

Stuart, E. W., Shimp, T. A., and Engle, R. W. (1987). Classical conditioning of consumer attitudes: Four experiments in an advertising context. Journal of Consumer Research, 14: 334–349.

Repeated Measures ANOVA

The repeated measures ANOVA indicates statistically significant effects (at the .05 level) of Drink and Imagery. Furthermore, the interaction between Drink and Imagery is also statistically significant (at the .05 level).
