Big Five Personality Traits: Pearson's Correlation

Description:

This data set, "Big Five Personality Traits", provides scores of 500 participants on a Big Five personality questionnaire.

Variables:

  1. Neuroticism - Score on the Neuroticism scale.
  2. Extraversion - Score on the Extraversion scale.
  3. Openness - Score on the Openness to Experience scale.
  4. Agreeableness - Score on the Agreeableness scale.
  5. Conscientiousness - Score on the Conscientiousness scale.

This example JASP file demonstrates the use of Pearson's correlation. We will explore the correlations between the five personality traits.

Reference: 

Dolan, C. V., Oort, F. J. Stoel, R. D, and Wicherts, J. M, (2009) Testing measurement invariance in the target rotated multigroup exploratory factor model, Structural Equation Modeling: A Multidisciplinary Journal, 16, 295-314.  

Correlation

Many correlations are statistically significant, although none are very high. The highest absolute correlation is between Neuroticism and Conscientiousness, where r = -.368. Below is also a correlation plot, which dispays the marginal distributions (on the diagonal), the pairwise scatterplots (in the top-right panels), and the associated statistics (in the bottom-left panels).
