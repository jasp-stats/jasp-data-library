Results

Description:

This data set, "Climate Change Disasters", studies the effect of news about a disaster framed as the cause of climate change on the justification to help the disaster victims.

Variables:

  • ID: Unique subject identifier.
  • Framing: Whether the disaster in the news article was framed as being cause by climate change or not.
  • DonationAttitude: Attitude towards donating score (higher score indicates more positive attitude).
  • Justification: Justification to withhold donations score.
  • Skepticism: Climate change skepticism score.

Each case represents one subject.

Reference:

Hayes, A. F. (2022). Introduction to mediation, moderation, and conditional process analysis (3rd Ed.). New York: The Guilford Press.

Chapman, D. A., & Lickel, B. (2016). Climate change and disasters: How framing affects justifications for giving or withholding aid to disaster victims. Social Psychological and Personality Science, 7, 13-20. https://doi.org/10.1177/1948550615590448

Path plots

The conceptual path plot shows the hypothesized moderation model. In this example, we assume that Skepticism moderates the effect of Framing on Justification. That is, the effect of framing the disaster as the cause of climate change on justification for withholding aid depends on climate change skepticism.

The statistical path plot shows the estimated statistical model. Whereas the conceptual path plot does not include interaction terms and dispalys moderation effects as paths pointing to another path, the statistical plot shows interaction terms and the direct effect of Skepticism on Justification.

The second table includes the effect of Framing on Justification probed at different percentiles of Skepticism. The results show that at high scores of Skepticism the effect of Framing on Justification is positive and significant, whereas there is no evidence for an effect at medium and low scores. Conclusion:

The moderation model showed evidence for an effect of climate change framing on the justification to withhold donations, but only for highly climate-change-skeptic subjects.
