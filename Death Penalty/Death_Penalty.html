Death Penalty: Bayesian Log-linear Regression

Description:

This data set, "Death Penalty", provides court decisions on a death penalty (i.e., yes or no) split by race of a defendant (i.e., White or Black) and race of the victim (i.e., White or Black).

Variables:

  • RaceVictim - Race of the victim (white, black).
  • RaceDefendant - Race of the defendant (white, black).
  • Count - The number of trials belonging to the combination of the categories.
  • Death - The court decision on the death penalty (yes = death penalty, no = no deatch penalty).

This example JASP file demonstrates the use of Bayesian log-linear analysis. The research question is: "Are racial characteristics associated with death penalty decisions?". Thus, we will test the three-way interaction.

Reference:

Radelet, M. L., and Pierce, G. L. (1991). Choosing those who will die: Race and the death penalty in Florida. Florida Law Review, 43: 1-34.
