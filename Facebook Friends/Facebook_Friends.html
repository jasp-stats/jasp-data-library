Facebook Friends: One-way ANOVA


Descriptive Statistics

The Descriptives menu allows us to get a first overview of the data. The Descriptive Statistics table displays the means, standard deviations, variances, minima, and maxima of the social attractiveness ratings in the five different experimental groups. We can see that the mean social attractiveness rating differs between the groups. On a descriptive level, it seems that there really is a curvilinear relationship: The mean social attractiveness rating for 102 and 902 friends is lower than the mean social attractiveness rating for the middle categories. This can also be seen in the boxplot figure below. From the figure, it also becomes very clear that the variance of attractiveness ratings differs between groups.

ANOVA

The table displays the results of the One-way ANOVA. It contains the Between(Friends)- and Within(Residual)-Group Variances, the degrees of freedom, the mean sum of squares, the F-test test statistic, the p-value of the F-test, and the effect size eta2. The results show that it is very unlikely to observe the mean differences (or more extreme) if the null hypothesis is true. The F-test is significant, F(4,129) = 4.142, p = 0.003. The effect size indicates that 11.4% of the variance in social attractiveness ratings can be explained through the number of friends.
