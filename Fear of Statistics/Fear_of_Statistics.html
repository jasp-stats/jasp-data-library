Fear of Statistics: Reliability (Internal Consistency) Analysis

Description:

This fictional dataset, "Fear of Statistics", provides responses of 2571 students on SPSS Anxiety Questionnaire.

All items are 5-point Likert scale (1 = strongly disagree, 5 = strongly agree).

This analysis is contingent on the exploratory factor analysis presented by Andy Field (2017) - that is, we have already identified which items belong to the Fear of Statistics subscale and will compute its reliability (in terms of internal consistency). The items of interest are:

Variables:

  1. Q01 - Statistics makes me cry
  2. Q03 - Standard deviations excite me (reverse coded)
  3. Q04 - I dream that Pearson is attacking me with correlation coefficients
  4. Q05 - I don't understand Statistics
  5. Q12 - People try to tell you that SPSS makes statistics easier to understand but it doesn't
  6. Q16 - I weep openly at the mention of central tendency
  7. Q20 - I can't sleep for thoughts of eigenvectors
  8. Q21 - I wake under my duvet thinking that I am trapped under normal distribution

This example JASP file demonstrates the use of reliability analysis. We will assess the internal consistency of this subscale using Cronbach's alpha and Gutmann's lambda 6. We will also include item statistics to assess whether all items work well individually within the scale.

Reference:

Field, A. P. (2017). Discovering Statistics Using IBM SPSS Statistics (5th ed.). London: Sage. [Fictional data set]
