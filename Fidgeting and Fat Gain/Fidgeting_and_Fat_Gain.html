Correlation plot

The plot shows a negative relation between NEA and fat gain, that is, participants who had more non-excersise activity gained relatively little weight. In the next step, we will test this relationship.

Linear Regression

The model with NEA as a predictor explains about 60% of the variance of fat gain (see the Model Summary table).

The second row in the Coefficients table indicates the slope of the independent variable, that is, how much the dependent variable changes with the change of the independent variable. The linear model can thus be expressed as Fat Gain = 3.505 - 0.003 * NEA.

The other values in the table summarize the coefficients. We can see that the regression coefficient of NEA is a statistically significant predictor of the Fat Gain with a 95% CI that ranges from -0.005 to -0.002.
