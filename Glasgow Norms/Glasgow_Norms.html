Glasgow Norms: Bayesian correlation

Description:

This data set, "Glasgow Norms", provides set of normative ratings for 5,553 English words (https://psyarxiv.com/akzyx/). They are rated on 9 psycholinguistic dimensions: Arousal (AROU), Valence (VAL), Dominance (DOM), Concreteness (CNC), Imageability (IMAG), Age of Acquisition (AOA), Semantic Size (SIZE), and Gender Association (GEND). For every given subset of words, the same participants provided ratings across all 9 dimensions.

This example JASP file demonstrates the use of Bayesian correlations. We are interested in the correlation between the dimensions. For example, are words that are rated higher on the Dominance dimension rated as more masculine or feminine? Or do words that are connected to high arousal have rather a positive or a negative valence? For this purpose, we have a look at the Bayesian Correlation Matrix.

Reference:

Scott, G. G., Keitel, A., Becirspahic, M., O'Donnel, P. J., Sereno, S. C. (2017). The Glasgow Norms: Ratings of 5,500 words on 9 scales. PsyArXiv Preprints: https://psyarxiv.com/akzyx/.

Bayesian Correlation

We generated a Bayesian Correlation Matrix for Pearson correlations. It becomes obvious from the correlation matrix that most dimensions are correlated. Apart from three correlations (AOA-AROU, SIZE-FAM, CNC-DOM), all Bayes factors show strong evidence for the existence of a correlation between the respective dimensions. Additionally, we can see that the magnitude of the correlations varies to a great extent. While some correlations are as large as r = .91 (IMAG-CNC), others are as small as .06 (CNC-VAL). That there is still strong evidence for the existence of an effect, even for these small correlations, can be explained by the large sample size of the study.
