Hungry Zombies: Bayesian Binomial Test

Description:

This fictional data set, "Hungry Zombies", provides answers of 12 zombies to the question: "what is your appetitive state?".

Variable:

  1. Outcome - answer of a zombie to the question about its appetitive state (Hungry = hungry).

This annotated JASP file showcases the Bayesian binomial test. An associated video is available through the JASP YouTube channel. The example, discussed in the JASP manual (in prep), is as follows. Consider Sunday Mourning, a girl with unusual interests who needs to prepare for her in-class presentation "Hangry? The Quintessential Zombie PR Problem". As part of the assignment, Sunday needs to discuss whether all zombies are hungry. Still lacking the relevant biological background to address this question theoretically, Sunday decides to approach the issue empirically, by visiting zombies and keeping track of how many are hungry.

Our example data set features the first 12 zombies that encountered. All of them are undeniably hungry. How much evidence is this that in general, "all zombies are hungry"? Clearly this law gains plausibility with every hungry zombie that is encountered, whereas the presence of a single satiated zombie refutes the law decisively. Let's make this more concrete by a Bayesian analysis. We have a case of k=12 hungry zombies out of a total of n=12 observed zombies. Under the general law, the rate parameter of interest is theta=1 (i.e., only hungry zombies are predicted to occur).
