Results

Description:

This data set, Imagery and Memory, contains variables about the effect of imagery on word recall. Subjects were assigned randomly to a treatment before hearing a sequence of words. After a short interval, they were asked to write down the words they could remember.

Variables:

  • StudyID - Identifier of the study (1-8).
  • Treatment - Randomized treatment where subjects where asked to repeat the words after seeing them (0) or make mental images of them (1).
  • Repitition - Answer to the question "I repeated the words over and over" (1-9).
  • WordsRecall - Number of correctly recalled words (out of 20).
  • Imagery - Answer to the question "I made mental images of the words" (1-9).

References:

MacKinnon, D. P., Valente, M. J., & Wurpts, I. C. (2014). Data from experimental studies of a rehearsal manipulation on imagery and word recall. Research in Prevention Laboratory Technical Report. Retrieved from https://psychology.asu.edu/research/labs/research-in-prevention-lab-mackinnon

Note that the results differ from the ones reported in this paper due to different modeling choices and estimation procedure.

The credible interval of the direct effect of Treatment includes zero suggest that there is no different between the two levels of Treatment in WordsRecall. The indirect effect of Treatment on WordsRecall through Imagery is positive and its credible interval excludes zero. This suggests that the effect of the treatment on the word record is fully mediated by the imagery score.

Conclusion:

The conditional process model showed evidence for a full mediation effect: That is, the treatment that subjects were supposed to make mental images of the words they heard increased the mental imagery score which in turn increased the number of words recalled.
