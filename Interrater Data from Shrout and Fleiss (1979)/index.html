Interrater Data from Shrout and Fleiss (1979)

Description:

Hypothetical data set from a paper on ICCs by Shrout and Fleiss (1979). We assume 6 subjects have been rated by 4 raters. The scale is (unfortunately) unknown.

Variables:

  • Rater.1: Ratings of rater nr. 1
  • Rater.2: Ratings of rater nr. 2
  • Rater.3: Ratings of rater nr. 3
  • Rater.4: Ratings of rater nr. 4

This example demonstrates the use of a classical intraclass correlation coefficient to estimate the agreement between raters. Here, we assume that all subjects have been rated by the same fixed set of raters.

References:

Shrout, P. E., & Fleiss, J. L. (1979). Intraclass correlations: Uses in assessing rater reliability. Psychological Bulletin, 86(2), 420–428. https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.86.2.420

Intraclass Correlation

We would like to know the agreement between the raters. We assume all subjets have been rated by the same fixed set of raters.

Note.  6 subjects and 4 raters/measurements. ICC type as referenced by Shrout & Fleiss (1979).

Conclusion:

The agreement is fairly good (ICC = .715). However the confidence limits indicate that there is a huge uncertainty associated with this estimation, meaning the actual agreement could be much higher or lower.
