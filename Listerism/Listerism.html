Listerism: Bayesian Contingency Tables

Description:

This data set, "Listerism", provides recovery status of patients treated with either Lister's method or ordinary dressings.

Variables:

  1. Method - Type of a treatment (Lister = Lister's method, Standard = Ordinary dressings).
  2. Outcome - Recovery status (Did Well, Blood Poisoning)
  3. Count - The number of patients falling into a specific category.

This example JASP file demonstrates the use of Bayesian contingency tables.

Reference:

Jamil, T., Marsman, M., Ly, A., Morey, R. D., & Wagenmakers, E.-J. (2017). What are the odds? Modern relevance and Bayes factor solutions for MacAlister's problem from the 1881 Educational Times. Educational and Psychological Measurement, 77: 819-830.
