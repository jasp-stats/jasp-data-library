Lookr or Personality: Mixed 2 (Between Subjects) x 3 (Within Subjects) x 3 (Within Subjects) Design ANOVA


Repeated Measures ANOVA

The mixed design ANOVA can be conducted using the Repeated Measures ANOVA analysis window in JASP. One has to specify the factors and levels of the repeated measures by hand, while the between subjects group (Gender) can be added into the Between Subjects Factors box.

Higher Education Sales teamThe descriptives plots reveal the overall pattern of the data - males rate attractive partners highly, regardless of their charisma, and rate ugly partners poorly, regardless of their charisma. For men, charisma makes a difference only for average looking partners.

For females, the preferences are the opposite - highly charismatic partners are rated the highest regardless of their attractiveness, and partners withouth any charisma are rated the lowest, regardless of their attractiveness. Only for partners with some charisma does attractiveness matter.

NB. The statements that the factor does not matter for some subset of partners is merely a qualitative judgement based on eye-balling the plotted patterns. For a quantitative assessment one would need to compute a Bayes factor or change the point-null hypothesis to an interval-null hypothesis so that an equivalence test can be carried out.
