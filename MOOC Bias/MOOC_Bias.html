MOOC Bias: 2x8 Contingency Table

Description:

This data set, "MOOC Bias" provides response rate from MOOC lecturers to questions posed by eight constructed identities [i.e., gender (male, female) x race(White, Indian, Black, Chinese)]. 

Variables:

  • Answer - Has the question been answered? (Yes, No).
  • Group - Constructed identity on an online course forum (WM = white male, WF = white female, IM = indian male, IF = indian female, BM = black male, BF = black female, CM = chinese male, CF = chinese female).
  • Count - The number of trials falling into the combination of categories.

This example JASP file demonstrates the use of a chi-squared test of independence. Specifically, we will assess the adequacy of the null hypothesis that all identities have the same probability of getting an answer to a question on an online course.

Reference:

Baker, R., Dee, T., Evans, B., and John., J. (2018). Bias in online classes: Evidence from a field experiment. Working paper, https://cepa.stanford.edu/content/bias-online-classes-evidence-field-experiment.

Contingency Tables

Below is the data summarised in a contingency table. The largest observed response rate was for white males, 15 out of 124 (12 %) of the questions posed by the white male identity were answered. On the other hand, the smallest observed response rate was for indian males, as only 4 out of 124 (3 %) of the questions posed by the indian male identity were answered.
