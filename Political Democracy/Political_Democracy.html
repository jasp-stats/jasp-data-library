Political Democracy: Structural Equation Model

Description:

This data set, "Political Democracy", provides indicators of industrialization in 1960, and political democracy indicators in 1960 and 1965.

Variables:

  1. x1, ... x3 - Indicators of industrialization in 1960.
  2. y1, ... y4 - Indicators of political democracy in 1960.
  3. y5, ... y8 - Indicators of political democracy in 1965.

This example JASP file demonstrates the use of structural equation modeling. Specifically, our model assumes three latent variables: Industrialization in 1960, Political Democracy in 1960, and Political Democracy in 1965. We test whether Political Democracy in 1960 and 1965 is related to Industrialization, and that Political Democracy in 1960 predicts Political Democracy in 1965.

Syntax

Specifically, latent variable ind60 (industrialization 1960) is measured (indicated) by variables x1-x3; latent variable dem60 (democracy 1960) is measured by variables y1-y4; and latent variable dem65 (democracy in 1965) is measured by y5-y8. In addition, the syntax also specifies to estimate regressions between the latent variables. Variable ind60 predicts dem60, and ind60 togehter with dem60 predicts dem65. We also allow some residual variances to correlate: the residual variances of y1 and y5, y2 with both y4 and y6, y3 and y7, y4 and y8, y6 and y8.

For an original example of this analysis see http://lavaan.ugent.be/tutorial/sem.html

Reference:

Bollen (1979). Political Democracy and the Timing of Development. American Sociological Review, 44: 572:587.
