Presidents' Height: How to Test an Interval-Null Hypothesis

Description:

This data set, "Presidents' Height", provides heights of US presidents and their proportion of the popular vote.

Variables:

  • Height Ratio - Ratio of the height of the US President to that of the closest competitor.
  • Popular Vote - The popular vote in the presidential elections.

This example JASP file demonstrates how to conduct a interval-null hypothesis. Specifically, we will test the default alternative hypothesis (i.e., the correlation between height ratio and popular vote is assigned a uniform prior from -1 to 1) against the null hypothesis. However, instead of using a point "nill" hypothesis (rho = 0), we will assess an interval null hypothesis which states that the correlation is nearly zero, but does not need to be exactly zero.

For explanation of the topic, see a blog post written by E.J. Wagenmakers at https://jasp-stats.org/2017/10/25/test-interval-null-hypotheses-jasp/

Reference:

Stulp, G., Buunk, A. P., Verhulst, S., & Pollet, T. V. (2013). Tall claims? Sense and nonsense about the importance of height of US presidents. The Leadership Quarterly, 24: 159-171.

For concreteness, let's analyze the association between the height of US presidents (versus that of their closest competitors) and the proportion of the popular vote.
