Results

Description:

This data set, "Presumed Media Influence", studies the influence of news placement on intention formation through beliefs about other people's news reception (presumed media influence).

Variables:

  1. Age: Age of subject.
  2. Condition: The randomized placement of the news article (front page vs. internal page) about a potential sugar shortage.
  3. Gender: Gender of subject.
  4. Importance: Perceived importance score of the article topic.
  5. Intention: Intention to buy sugar score after reading the news article.
  6. MediaInfluence: Presumed media influence score, i.e., how much subjects believed others would buy sugar after reading the article.

Each case represents one subject. This example demonstrates the use of a simple mediation analysis with a dichotomous independent variable. It is also included in Hayes (2022, Chapter 3.3).

Reference:

Hayes, A. F. (2022). Introduction to mediation, moderation, and conditional process analysis (3rd Ed.). New York: The Guilford Press.

Tal-Or, N., Cohen, J., Tsafati, Y., & Gunther, A. C. (2010). Testing causal direction in the influence of presumed media influence. Communication Research, 37, 801-824. https://doi.org/10.1177/0093650210362684

Conclusion:

The simple mediation model showed a positive indirect effect of the placement of a news article about a potential sugar shortage on the front page on the intention to buy sugar via the presumed incfluence of the article on other readers intentions.
