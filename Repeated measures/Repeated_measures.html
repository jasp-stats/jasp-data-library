Plot Builder: Repeated measurements plot

This example shows how to use the Plot builder module to create repeated measurements plots.

Description:

This data set, "Weight Gain", provides weights of 16 participants before and after an eight-week period of excessive calorie intake (Moore et al., 2012, p. 425).

Variables:

  1. Weight Before - Weight in pounds (lb) measured before eight weeks of excessive calorie intake.
  2. Weight After - Weight in pounds (lb) measured after eight weeks of excessive calorie intake.


Using a paired t-test, we compared Before and After Weight measurements and found that the difference was not statistically significant (p = .325), suggesting that the intervention had no measurable effect on weight.


References:

Moore, D. S., McCabe, G. P., and Craig, B. A. (2012). Introduction to the Practice of Statistics (7th ed.). New York: Freeman.

Levine, J. A., Eberhardt, N. L., and Jensen, M. D. (1999) Role of nonexcercise activity thermogenesis in resistance to fat gain in humans. Science, 283:212-214.

Plot Builder (beta)

  1. Enable repeated measurements → Set this option to “Yes” to indicate that the same subjects were measured under different conditions.
  2. Rename the levels → In the “Repeated Measures Factors” window, rename Level 1 and Level 2 to reflect the measurement points (e.g., Before and After).
  3. Assign variables to measurement points → In the “Repeated Measures Cells” window, assign “Weight Before” and “Weight After” to the corresponding levels.
  4. Add individual data points → This option is available under “Individual data points.”
  5. Connect paired values → Since these are within-subject measurements, you can link the Before–After points by checking the “Connect data points” box.
  6. Edit the y-axis label → Change it to “Weight” under the “Y-Axis” settings.
  7. Edit the x-axis label → Change it to something meaningful, such as “Time Point”, under “X-Axis” settings.
  8. Add the p-value → Use the “P value brackets” option. Click the green plus button and specify the exact names of the groups to compare (e.g., Before vs After). Note: If the p-value appears cut off above the plot, increase the Top padding under “Size, margins and plot orientation.”
