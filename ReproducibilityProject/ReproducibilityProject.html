JAGS

Results for a mixture model. The replicated effect size is either


  1. Normally distributed with mean 0, if the null hypothesis is true (i.e., clust[i] equals 0).
  2. Normally distributed with mean alpha * the effect size in the original study.


The parameter "alpha" is the regression coefficient when regressing the replicated effect size on that of the original study, under the assumption that the null hypothesis in the original study was false. Despite ignoring replications where the null is assumed to be false, the posterior mean of alpha is smaller than 1. This indicates that the effect sizes in the original studies are probably overestimated.
