Stereograms: Bayesian Independent Samples T-Test

Description:

This data set, "Stereograms", provides the time it took two groups of participants to see a figure hidden in a stereogram - one group received advance information about the scene, the other group did not.

Variables:

  1. V1 - Participant number.
  2. fuseTime - the time (in seconds) it took the participant to see the hidden figure.
  3. condition - experimental condition (NV = without information, VV = with information)
  4. logFuseTime - the log transformation of the fuseTime

This example JASP file demonstrates the use of Bayesian independent samples t-test. Specifically, we compare the null hypothesis (providing participants with information has no effect on the fuse times) with a one-sided alternative hypothesis (providing participants with information about the hidden figure shortens the time participant needs to see the figure).

Reference:

Frisby, J. P. and Clatworthy, J. L. (1975). Learning to see complex random-dot stereograms. Perception, 4:173-178.
