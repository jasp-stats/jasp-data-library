The Biggest Liar: Ordinal Measures of Association

Description:

This fictional data set, "The Biggest Liar", provides participants' creativity test scores and their rankings in a lying competition.

Variables:

  1. Creativity - The creativity test score.
  2. Position - The ranking achieved in the Biggest Liar contest.
  3. Novice - Is the person participating for the first time (1) or not (0)?

This example JASP file demonstrates the use of Spearman's and Kendall's correlation. Specifically, we will assess the adequacy of the null hypothesis which posits that creativity is uncorrelated to the rank in the lying contest.

Reference:

Field, A. P. (2017). Discovering Statistics Using IBM SPSS Statistics (5th ed.). London: Sage. [Fictional data set]

The Position variable is ordinal (and skewed), suggesting the use of a rank-based measure of association such as Spearman's rho or Kendall's tau. 

Correlation

Both the coefficients (Spearman's rho = -0.373, Kendall's tau = -0.300) suggest that Creativity correlates negatively with Position -- that is, creative people tend tend to perform better in the lying competition (NB: lower ranks signify better performance). The observed correlations (or more extreme) are relatively unlikely under the null hypothesis: p=.002 for Spearman's rho and p=.001 for Kendall's tau.
