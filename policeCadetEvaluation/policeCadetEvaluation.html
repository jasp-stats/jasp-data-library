Decision Tree Classification

The objective of this analysis is to see if requirements for passing the standard five-year police cadet evaluation could be predicted based on data collected at the time of graduation of police cadets. The primary reason for the study is to find key indicators for the then 20% failure rate, which was considered unacceptable (data were collected in the late 1990s).

The data contains 2000 observations of 9 features (including one class, and one help attribute):


  1. Age: the age at which the cadet started studying to become a police officer
  2. AvgG: average grade at the time of graduation (scale 1-10)
  3. Chdn: number of children at the time of graduation
  4. ExEd: extra university-level or equivalent education (years)
  5. CR: criminal record (0 = No, 1 = Yes)
  6. Sex: sex of the cadet (0 = Male, 1 = Female)
  7. SecE: other experience in the security sector (0 = No, 1 = Yes)
  8. AvgE: average yearly evaluation score (The average of five years. The evaluation is performed by a committee of 10 senior police officers. Scale 1-5). This is a help attribute and not for use as input.
  9. FinalE: final evaluation. Fail if average yearly evaluation score (Avg) < 2.0 otherwise pass. This is the target attribute.

Note that not all variables are ethically appropriate to use. For example, it would be highly inappropriate to fire a police cadet on the basis of their sex alone. Hence, some variables are forcefully excluded from the predictive model.

Source: Colijn, van Pienhoven. Using Bayesian statistics in forecasting police performance. 8th International Conference on IT security and law enforcement, 2000.
